Reflection:

Responsive Design is a method of combining HTML and CSS to automatically resize, hide, shrink, or enlarge a website, to make it look aesthetically pleasing across all devices (desktops, tablets, and mobile phones). It produces a website that has a fluid grid, fluid media, and also a responsive media query. It also considers a careful selection of methods to make sure that the website will be displayed properly on older browsers. This means that not every new framework such as FlexBox or Bootstrap should be adopted with every new website, as it may limit browser compatibility depending on the version of the browser. Web technologies will benefit a lot from responsive design since more business processes are being done digitally these days. It is crucial for users to maintain their connections and maintain productivity through responsive design. From the initial landing page, to a web app, educational platform, tele-conferencing or an e-commerce store, more people are relying on digital solutions to integrate their day to day lives in school or work. To ensure a smooth and pleasurable experience for everyone, it is highly important to have a responsive design which increases productivity, aesthetics, and both business and users will gain a lot of value from it. For this particular e-commerce project, I chose to study “How Percentages Work for Width Calculations in a Responsive Website”. I learned how percentage values can create a truly fluid grid since both elements and the images / texts contained within the corresponding elements will behave in relative positioning to each other.
